iClaw cloud

End-to-end encrypted chat sharing

Companion cloud for iClaw. Shared chats are encrypted in your browser with AES-256-GCM and uploaded as opaque bytes. The server stores ciphertext only — keys live in the URL fragment (after #) and never reach us.

Every share has a TTL (1, 3, 7 or 30 days) and can optionally be protected with a password and / or set to self-destruct after the first view.

How to share a chat: in iClaw, open a chat, click Share, pick a TTL, copy the link.

What we don't see

  • The chat text — only encrypted bytes.
  • The decryption key — it lives in your URL fragment.
  • The password, if you set one — never leaves your browser.

What we do see

  • The id of each share, its size, and its TTL.
  • A hash of the creator IP (kept for abuse responses only).
  • Read counts (so burn-after-read works).
